# Bidding

Updated: Dec 8, 2025

Learn how your bids and budget work with Facebook's ad auction and delivery. This covers bidding options, placing bids for desired action, setting budget limits and tracking ads delivery. Facebook's auction functions the same way for API-created ads as for ads from Facebook tools.

## Main Concepts

- **Bid Strategies** — Provide your bid preferences. (/bidding/overview/bid-strategy)
- **Optimization Goals** — Define advertising goals you want to achieve when Facebook delivers your ads. (/bidding/overview#opt)
- **Budgets** (/bidding/overview/budgets)
- **Pacing and Scheduling** — Determine how your ads budget is spent over time. (/bidding/overview/pacing-and-scheduling)
- **Billing Events** — Defines events you want to pay for: impressions, clicks, or various actions. (/bidding/overview/billing-events)

## Common Use Cases

- **Campaign Budget Optimization** — Optimize distribution of a campaign budget across ad sets. (/marketing-api/bidding-and-optimization/campaign-budget-optimization)
- **Optimized Cost Per Mille Ads** — Prioritize marketing goals; automatically deliver ads toward them most effectively. (/bidding/guides/optimized-cost-per-mille)
- **Cost Per Action Ads** — Specify conversion events and get charged by amount of conversions. (/bidding/guides/cost-per-action-ads)
- **Reach and Frequency** — Bid on a predicted unique audience reach and control display frequency. (/marketing-api/reachandfrequency)
- **Bid Multipliers** — Maintain a nuanced bidding strategy within a single ad set with one targeted audience. Available on a limited basis. (/marketing-api/bidding-and-optimization/bid-multiplier)
